     We are interested in agricultural genomics, a multidisciplinary field at the interface of agriculture, biology and computer science. This young and fascinating field attracts us as a young group also, with member ages between 20-40. We run a functional genomics facility for sequencing, assembly, annotation and analysis of genomes and transcriptomes of tropical and subtropical species of plants and animals. An integrated platform has been developed which allowed us to do the genome analysis jobs efficiently. A gene mining platform was developed which made it possible to identify specific genes from millions of sequences within seconds precisely. Through our wet lab facilities, functional characterization and biotechnological work could be carried out to push the data into the realm of reality.

     Currently, we are interested in the following questions:

     (1) How genomic innovation and adaptation shaped great diversity of Solanaceae around the current globe?

     (2) What made Anacardiaceae plants so unique in their fruits and natural products accumulated in the plant bodies, and how can we alter genomic compositions to avoid production of hazardous metabolites?

     (3) How genome evolution facilitated emergence and innovation of traits in eukaryotes.
